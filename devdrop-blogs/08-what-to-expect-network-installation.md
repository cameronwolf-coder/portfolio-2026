# What to Expect During a Professional Network Installation

You know your business needs a better network. Maybe you're dealing with slow WiFi, dropped connections, or an aging setup held together with consumer-grade equipment and good luck. Maybe you're moving into a new space. Either way, you've decided it's time to bring in a professional.

But if you've never been through a commercial network installation, you probably have questions. How long does it take? Will it shut down my business? What happens if something goes wrong after the installer leaves?

At DevDrop, we've completed over 500 network projects for more than 200 businesses across West Michigan. We've refined our process over 15 years to make sure every project goes smoothly, stays on budget, and delivers exactly what we promised. Here's a complete walkthrough of what to expect.

## Step 2: Site Survey and Proposal -- Assessing Your Space

Once we have a clear picture of your needs, we schedule a site survey. This is where one of our technicians visits your location and does a thorough assessment of your physical space.

### What Happens During a Site Survey

We walk through your entire facility looking at building construction, existing infrastructure, network closet options, device locations, interference sources, and cable pathways. A concrete warehouse is a very different environment than a modern open-plan office, and each affects how we design your network.

We document everything with notes, photos, and measurements. This is the foundation of your network design.

### The Written Proposal

After the site survey, we put together a detailed written proposal that includes a complete scope of work, a list of all equipment and materials, a clear timeline, and **transparent pricing with no hidden fees.**

When we say "no surprises," we mean it. The price on the proposal is the price you pay. If something changes during installation that affects scope or cost, we talk to you about it before we proceed.

You'll have time to review the proposal and ask questions. We're happy to walk through every line item. Once you approve, we schedule the installation.

## Step 3: Professional Installation -- We Handle Everything

This is where the real work happens. Our technicians arrive at your location and execute the plan we've laid out in the proposal. Here's what installation day (or days) actually looks like.

### Cabling

Structured cabling is the foundation of your network. Our technicians run Cat6 or Cat6A cable from your network closet to every device location in your facility. Every cable is routed cleanly through walls, ceilings, or conduit -- never just draped across the floor or zip-tied to a pipe.

Each cable run is terminated at both ends: at a wall jack near the device location and at a patch panel in your network closet. Every single run is labeled clearly so that anyone -- including you -- can identify which cable goes where.

**Clean racks, clean results.** We take pride in cable management. When we're finished, your network closet looks organized and professional, not like a bowl of spaghetti.

### Hardware Installation

We mount and install all the network equipment specified in your proposal. This typically includes:

- **Switches** that connect all your wired devices
- **Firewall/router** that protects your network and manages internet traffic
- **Wireless access points** positioned for optimal coverage
- **UPS (battery backup)** to keep your network running during power interruptions
- **Security cameras and NVR** if your project includes surveillance
- **Patch panels** that organize your cable terminations
- **Rack and cable management** to keep everything neat and accessible

### Configuration

Hardware is only half the story. Our technicians handle all configuration on-site: network segmentation, firewall rules, wireless network setup, VPN configuration, and quality of service settings for VoIP phones and video conferencing.

You don't need to understand any of these terms. We configure everything according to best practices and your specific business requirements.

### Working Around Your Schedule

We know that a network installation happening in the middle of your workday is disruptive. That's why we work around your schedule as much as possible.

For businesses that can't afford any downtime, we can do the bulk of the cabling and hardware installation during off-hours -- evenings, weekends, or holidays. The final cutover from your old network to your new one can often be done in a matter of hours during a planned maintenance window.

For new spaces where there's no existing business to disrupt, we coordinate with your general contractor and other trades to stay on schedule and out of each other's way.

**Most projects are completed within one to three weeks**, depending on the size and complexity of the installation. A small office might take just a few days. A multi-floor commercial building could take two to three weeks. We'll give you a clear timeline in your proposal so you know exactly what to expect.

## Step 4: Testing and Handoff -- Making Sure Everything Works

We don't consider a project finished just because the equipment is installed. Every DevDrop project ends with a thorough testing and handoff process.

### Cable Certification

Every single cable run is tested and certified using professional testing equipment. If a cable doesn't pass, we fix it before we leave. You'll receive test results as part of your project documentation.

### Network Testing

Beyond cabling, we test the entire network as a system: internet connectivity and speed, wireless coverage in every area, VoIP call quality, VPN connectivity, firewall security, camera system functionality, and device connectivity for every workstation, printer, phone, and camera.

### Full Documentation

When your project is complete, you receive a documentation package including a network diagram, equipment inventory with serial numbers and warranty info, cable run schedule, configuration details, and test results. This documentation is yours to keep -- if you ever need to troubleshoot or make changes, everything is documented clearly.

### The Walkthrough

Before we pack up and leave, we sit down with you (or your office manager, or whoever manages IT day to day) and walk through everything: where your equipment is, what each piece does, how to access your wireless networks, and basic troubleshooting steps for common issues.

Our goal is to make sure **you understand how your network works** and feel confident. We don't use jargon, and we don't rush. If you have questions, we answer them.

We know you have concerns. Here are the ones we hear most often.
