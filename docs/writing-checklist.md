# Human Writing Pre-Commit Check

Before committing written content, sanity-check the following:

## Structure
- [ ] Does it start in the middle, not with throat-clearing?
- [ ] Are there fewer than 3 main points? (More = over-explaining)
- [ ] No "In conclusion" or neat wrap-up?

## Language
- [ ] No em-dashes?
- [ ] No "Here's the thing" or similar cliche transitions?
- [ ] Adjectives replaced with numbers where possible?
- [ ] No motivational speaker energy?

## Substance
- [ ] States what this does NOT solve?
- [ ] Acknowledges at least one open question or risk?
- [ ] Shows what actually happened, not just what was planned?

## Tone
- [ ] Sounds like thinking out loud, not a presentation?
- [ ] Vulnerable without being self-pitying?
- [ ] Has at least one moment of humor or lightness?

## Time & Reality
- [ ] Anchored to specific constraints, data, or priorities?
- [ ] Ends with "for now" energy, not false certainty?
- [ ] Would you actually say this out loud?

---

**If more than 2 boxes feel forced, rewrite before committing.**
